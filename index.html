About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of California, Berkeley working with Prof. A. Paul Alivisatos . I received my PhD from Rice University advised by Prof. Matteo Pasquali in 2017. Throughout my PhD, I worked on a model system of anisotropic nanomaterials (carbon nanotubes) and studied the entropy-driven self assembly of these nanomaterial building blocks into liquid crystalline phases in order to assemble them into macromaterials. As a postdoc, I have used fluid-phase processing to develop functional materials with unique optoelectronic properties that can be actuated at the macroscale. Currently, I am using in-situ liquid cell transmission electron microscopy to study the dynamics of nanomaterials in collaboration with Prof. Kranthi Mandadapu .

Curriculum Vitae

Research

My research interest lies at the interface of soft matter, nanoscience, and material processing. I aim to use nanomaterial building blocks and develop smart active materials at different length scale.

  • In-situ study of the dynamics of colloidal nanoparticles at the nanoscale

  • Experimental and theoretical study to understand and design bottom-up organization of nanoparticles into ordered liquid crystalline phases

  • Fluid phase processing of nanomaterial building blocks into smart functional macroscale materials with targeted applications
