About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of California, Berkeley working with Prof. A. Paul Alivisatos and Prof. Kranthi Mandadapu . My research is focused on connecting nanoscopic features to micro and macroscopic phenomena in soft matter systems. I use experimental, theoretical, and computational tools such as in-situ liquid cell electron microscopy, rheology, statistical and colloidal thermodynamics, and artificial intelligence to study the structure and dynamics of nanostructured soft matter systems. I received my PhD from Rice University advised by Prof. Matteo Pasquali in 2017, where I worked on nano and microstructured colloidal complex fluid systems and interfaces as well as material processing. In particular, I studied a model system of anisotropic nanomaterials (carbon nanotubes) and their entropy-driven self-assembly into liquid crystalline phases. My current research focuses on using in-situ liquid cell transmission electron microscopy in combination with machine learning and theoretical tools from Langevin dynamics to study the complex anomalous diffusive motion of a model system of nanoparticles in boundary layers and how interactions of nanoparticles with each other as well as the surface influence their motion.
